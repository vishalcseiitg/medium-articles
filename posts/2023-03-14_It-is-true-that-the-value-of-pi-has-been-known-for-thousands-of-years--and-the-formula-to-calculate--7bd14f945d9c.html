It is true that the value of pi has been known for thousands of years, and the formula to calculate pi is well established. However, there are still reasons why researchers may want to calculate pi to an extremely high degree of accuracy, such as 1.24 trillion decimal places.

One of the main reasons for calculating pi to such a high degree of accuracy is to test the performance of supercomputers and high-precision algorithms. These calculations require a tremendous amount of computing power and memory, and testing the limits of current technology can help researchers develop even more powerful systems.

In addition, high-precision calculations of pi can also be used in fields such as cryptography and number theory, where precise calculations of mathematical constants are important.

Furthermore, such calculations can also serve as a benchmark or challenge for mathematicians and computer scientists to test their abilities and push the boundaries of their fields.

While the value of pi is already known to a high degree of accuracy, calculating it to an even higher degree can still have practical and academic value, especially in fields related to computer science and mathematics.
